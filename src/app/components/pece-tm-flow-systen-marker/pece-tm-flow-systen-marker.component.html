The Heart Rate Variability (HRV) markers are measured in 2 minutes-time. The ARIC study validated the HRV 2 minute- time measurement and the normal ranges associated with this measurement time.

TOTAL POWER

Total Power is calculated by the spectral analysis of the heart rate variability in 2 minutes at rest. It is a measure of the overall activity of the autonomic nervous system at rest and ensures the maintenance of the autonomic homeostatic function at rest.

SDANN STANDS FOR STANDARD DEVIATION AVERAGE NORMAL TO NORMS (RR INTERVALS)

SDANN is calculated from the time domain of the HRV in 2 minutes at rest. It is a measure of both sympathetic and parasympathetic system activity.

CARDIAC AUTONOMIC NEUROPATHY READING GUIDANCE

CARTs are the “Gold” standard clinical tests for cardiovascular autonomic neuropathy. In the CAN Subcommittee of the Toronto Consensus Panel statement are defined criteria for CAN definition and severity. For the early CAN diagnosis, only one abnormal CART result (among the 7 tests: 5 CARTs and HRV tests in time- and frequency-domains) is sufficient; definite CAN should be confirmed by 2 or 3 abnormal tests and severe CAN can be indicated by the development of OH.

SUDMOTOR TESTS : DIABETIC OR SMALL FIBER NEUROPATHY DIAGNOSTIC MARKERS

Sudomotor testing assesses the postganglionic cholinergic sympathetic vasodilatory response to predetermined stimulation which induces the activation of the cholinergic fiber and sweat release. The postganglionic cholinergic sympathetic vasodilatory response (PCSVR marker NO peak) is evaluated at the negative electrode and the density of the active cholinergic fiber according to the sweat production response marker Sweat Peak) is evaluated at the positive electrode. The measured responses are displayed in voltage (mV). Normal ranges were established on more than 20,000 Healthy subject databases.

LEFT AND RIGHT FOOT NO PEAK STANDS FOR NITRIC OXIDE PEAK

NO Peak is calculated from the peak amplitude of the galvanic skin response at the negative electrode. It is a measure of the postganglionic cholinergic sympathetic vasodilatory response.

LEFT AND RIGHT FOOT SWEAT PEAK

Sweat Peak is calculated from the peak amplitude of the galvanic skin response at the positive electrode. It is a measure of the density of the active cholinergic fiber according to the sweat production response.

NERVES AFFECTED AND TESTING FOR PERIPHERAL DISTAL NEUROPATHY

Pain may be caused by any dysfunction or damage of Peripheral nerve (motor nerve) and/or Aα fiber (large fiber) and /or Aδ (sensory nerve) and/or C-Fiber Autonomic cholinergic sympathetic fiber controlling the sudomotor function)

Used diagnostic tools:

The vibration tests are used to detect the Aα fiber dysfunction or damage. The monofilament tests are used to detect the Aδ fiber dysfunction or damage. The nerve conduction velocity studies are used to detect the Peripheral nerve dysfunction or damage.

The sudomotor tests are used to detect the C-Fiber dysfunction or damage. The C-Fibers are unmyelinated. Therefore, they are not protected and usually should be the first ones to be affected before the other myelinated nerves.

SUDMOTOR TESTS READING GUIDANCE

The Lewis at al. study [15]shown that the NO peak marker can be useful for detecting microvascular diseases. The marker NO Peak inversely correlated with BUN (ρ = −0.41, p < 0.0001), homocysteine (ρ = −0.44, p < 0.0001), fibrinogen (ρ = −0.41, p < 0.0001), Receiver operating characteristic curve of NO Peak. NO Peak had a sensitivity of 88% and specificity of 68% (cut-off score ≤ 64) with an area under the curve (AUC) = 0.81 (SE = 0.04; 95% CI = 0.72, 0.88) and an asymptotic significance <0.0001). Sweat Peak inversely correlated with the severity of symptoms on the peripheral neuropathy scale (ρ = −0.56, p < 0.0001).

ENDOTHELIAL FUNCTION READING GUIDANCE

The PTG spectral analysis provides oscillation called vasomotion or flowmotion. Vasomotion is defined as the spontaneous rhythmic contraction and dilation of the muscle components in the small blood vessel walls which generate rhythmic changes in their diameter.

Vasomotion could be measured by the Lase Doppler Flowmetry (LDF) as well as the photoplethysmography (PTG) using the spectral analysis of the generated wave of the microcirculatory blood volume changes.

The findings that ACh iontophoresis increased amplitude of the oscillation around 0.009–0.02 Hz to a greater extent than sodium nitroprusside iontophoresis or than ischemia suggested a role of the endothelium in the control of this flowmotion component. This role was confirmed by the observation that L-NMMA abolished this difference.

The normal ranges of the component of the vasomotion have been established from the clinical studies.

ABI STANDS FOR ANKLE BRACHIAL INDEX

ABI is calculated from the lowest systolic pressure (dorsalis Pedis and Posterior tibial artery) at each ankle divided the highest systolic pressure at arms. It is accepted as a diagnosis of the Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD) at the lower extremity and a marker of atherosclerosis.

SA AMPLITUDE STANDS FOR SPECTRAL ANALYSIS OF AMPLITUDE

SA Amplitude is calculated from the spectral analysis of the volume plethysmography recorded from the cuffs during the deflation during the blood pressure measurement at the arms and ankles. It is a marker of the blood flow recovery after artery occlusion.

ABI READING GUIDANCE

Eight studies assessed the diagnostic performances of an ABI <=0.90 to detect >= 50% stenosis identified by imaging methods, including color duplex ultrasound, magnetic resonance angiography, or angiography. All these studies found reasonably high specificity (83%–99%).

Ouriel et al Aboyans et al Measurement and Interpretation of the Ankle-Brachial Index 2893 reported an average ABI decrease of 5% from resting to post-exercise values after treadmill exercise in healthy people compared with 20% in patients with PAD.

To improve the precision of the test, it is reasonable to measure each limb pressure twice and to average the results of each artery to calculate the ABI (Class IIa; Level of Evidence C).
